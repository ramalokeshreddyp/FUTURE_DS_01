# Business Sales Performance Analytics
## Insights Report — Future Interns DS1 Task 1 · March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

This report presents findings from a comprehensive analysis of 5,000 business sales transactions spanning January 2021 to December 2024. The dataset covers three product categories (Technology, Furniture, Office Supplies), four US regions, and three customer segments. Total portfolio revenue stands at **$3.32M** with a net profit of **$534.7K** (18.26% average margin).

---

## 1. Revenue Trends

**Finding:** Revenue has grown steadily year-over-year at ~8–9% CAGR, rising from ~$700K in 2021 to ~$906K in 2024.

**Seasonality:** A clear Q4 spike is observed every year — November and December consistently outperform all other months, driven by holiday purchasing and year-end corporate procurement.

**Recommendation:**
- Launch dedicated Q4 email and social campaigns in October to capture early holiday demand.
- Pre-stock top-selling Technology SKUs before November to avoid stockouts during the peak.

---

## 2. Top-Selling Products

**Finding:** The top 5 revenue-generating products are all in Technology and Furniture:
1. Laptop — $396,200
2. Standing Desk — $278,400
3. Smartphone Pro — $253,100
4. Monitor — $231,500
5. Executive Chair — $197,800

Laptops account for ~11.9% of total Technology revenue alone. Wireless Mouse, while lower in unit price, ranks highly in volume and has a healthy 26% profit margin.

**Recommendation:**
- Bundle high-margin accessories (Wireless Mouse, USB-C Hub) with Laptop purchases to increase average order value.
- Create a "Home Office" bundle (Laptop + Ergonomic Chair + Desk) to cross-sell across categories.

## 3. Category Performance

| Category | Revenue | Profit | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Technology | $1,178,432 | $238,250 | **20.2%** |
| Office Supplies | $1,152,694 | $196,880 | **17.1%** |
| Furniture | $987,523 | $99,612 | **10.1%** |

**Finding:** Technology and Office Supplies are nearly tied in revenue, but Technology delivers double Furniture's profit margin. Furniture's thin margins (10.1%) suggest high fulfilment/shipping costs.

**Recommendation:**
- Investigate Furniture supplier contracts and shipping partners for cost reduction.
- Consider minimum-order quantity pricing for Furniture to improve per-unit economics.
- Office Supplies has high volume and stable margins — expand SKU depth here.

## 4. Regional Performance

| Region | Revenue | Profit | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| East | $1,024,500 | $185,200 | 18.1% |
| West | $958,300 | $168,900 | 17.6% |
| South | $730,400 | $112,300 | 15.4% |
| Central | $605,449 | $68,342 | 11.3% |

**Finding:** The East and West together account for 60% of revenue. Central region's 11.3% margin is the weakest, suggesting pricing or cost structure issues. South shows untapped growth potential.

**Recommendation:**
- Central region: Audit discount behaviour — over-discounting may be the root cause of margin compression.
- South region: Run geo-targeted digital campaigns; appoint regional account managers.
- East: Maintain momentum with loyalty and repeat-purchase programs.

---

## 5. Discount Impact

**Finding:** Orders with 0% discount yield average profits of ~$145.8. As discounts increase above 20%, average profit drops below $90 — at 30% discounts, profit falls to just $52.3 per order.

**Recommendation:**
- Cap automatic/blanket discounts at 15%. Any discount above 20% should require manager approval.
- Replace high-discount campaigns with value-add promotions (free shipping, extended warranty, bundled accessories).

---

## 6. Customer Segment Analysis

| Segment | Revenue Share | Profit | Avg Order Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer | 52.1% | $289,100 | High volume, moderate AOV |
| Corporate | 30.5% | $175,800 | Highest AOV, most profitable per order |
| Home Office | 17.4% | $69,842 | Underutilised segment |

**Finding:** Corporate customers represent 30.5% of revenue but yield the highest per-order profitability. Home Office is the smallest and fastest-growing segment opportunity.

**Recommendation:**
- Establish a dedicated B2B sales team for Corporate accounts, offering volume pricing and dedicated support.
- Create a "Home Office Starter Pack" product line targeting the remote-work market.

---

## 7. Shipping Mode Analysis

Standard Class carries ~60% of orders. First Class and Same Day modes, despite generating higher per-order values, represent only 20% combined.

**Recommendation:**
- Offer Standard Class with a "guaranteed 4-day" SLA — customers respond to reliability over speed promises.
- Upsell Same Day to Corporate segment for urgent procurement orders.
